1st International Workshop on Industrial Recommendation Systems

INTRO

Recommendation system has shown its power for optimizing user experience in not only eCommerce industries but also content serving companies in the past few years. This motivates the industrial facilities and research organizations or individuals to focus on the personalization and recommendation frameworks and algorithms, which results in the flourish of research papers on the related areas. While academia research on recommendation systems mostly focuses on the performance of the algorithm in terms of ranking quality or accuracy, it usually neglects a few key factors that are critical and usually determine the choice of the frameworks and models of a recommendation system in real world production. These key factors are including but not limited to, industrial-level scalability on both data and model, model serving latency, model interpretability, and resource limitations, such as budget on computing facilities, computing power of existing computing facilities, etc. The gap between the academia research and industry modeling in production makes the research publications from academy not trivially applicable to industrial recommendation systems, especially for those companies with limited resources and tech debts. This workshop focuses on bridging this gap by bringing it to the attention of researchers from both academy and industry, and motivates the researchers from both communities to jointly work on industrial recommendation algorithms by taking those key factors into consideration.

The gap between the practitioners and academia researchers on industrial recommendation systems has not been widely recognized or effectively addressed, given that there have been numerous conferences with topics focusing on or including recommendation systems, such as ICLR, Recsys, ICDM, SDM, CIKM, to name a few. With the fast development of related research areas, the requirement becomes more and more urgent to 1). attract more researchers from different areas on industrial recommendation systems, and 2). bring up the pain points in industry so that academia researchers can pay more attention and build tighter connections with practitioners. With the reputation of KDD conference and the attendance of audiences across various domains, it could be expected that this workshop will bring up considerable attention.

CALL FOR PAPER

This workshop welcomes submissions from researchers and industrial practitioners broadly related to recommendation systems, such as novel recommendation models, efficient recommendation algorithms, novel industrial frameworks, etc.. In order to emphasize the gap between the two communities, we extremely welcome submissions on industrial recommendation system infrastructures based on given resources, models and algorithms supported by the specific infrastructures, and frameworks or end-to-end systems that have been deployed in real world production.

Specific topics of interest are including but not limited to:

  1. Novel data mining and machine learning algorithms for scalable Recommender systems
  2. Personalization, including personalized product recommendation, ads recommendation, news and article recommendation, streaming content recommendation, etc.
  3. New applications related to recommendation systems.
  4. Existing or novel infrastructures for recommendation systems.
  5. Frameworks or end-to-end systems from industry are extremely welcome.
